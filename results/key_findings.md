# Key Findings

This file summarizes the main findings from the SQL analysis.


## 1. Countries with the highest life expectancy

In 2023, the highest life expectancy values were observed in Monaco, San Marino, Hong Kong, Japan, and South Korea. Monaco had the highest value in the dataset, with life expectancy above 86 years.

This suggests that several high-income or highly developed regions tend to have very high life expectancy.

## 2. Countries with the lowest life expectancy

In 2023, the lowest life expectancy values were observed in Nigeria, Chad, Lesotho, the Central African Republic, South Sudan, Somalia, and Mali.

Nigeria had the lowest life expectancy in this result, with approximately 54.46 years. Most countries in the bottom results are located in Africa, which suggests that life expectancy can be strongly affected by broader social, economic, healthcare, and stability-related factors.

## 3. Life expectancy trend in Czechia

Life expectancy in Czechia increased significantly between 1950 and 2023. In 1950, life expectancy was approximately 64.41 years, while in 2023 it reached approximately 79.83 years.

This represents an increase of about 15.42 years over the analyzed period. The long-term trend is strongly positive, although there was a visible short-term decline in 2020 and 2021, followed by a recovery in 2022 and 2023.

## 4. GDP per capita and life expectancy

The results suggest that countries with higher GDP per capita often have relatively high life expectancy. In 2023, countries and territories such as Luxembourg, Singapore, Ireland, Qatar, Norway, and Switzerland had both high GDP per capita and life expectancy above 82 years.

However, the relationship is not perfect. Some countries or territories with high GDP per capita, such as Brunei and Greenland, had lower life expectancy compared with other high-income countries. This suggests that GDP per capita is an important factor, but it is definitely not the only factor influencing life expectancy.

## 5. Average life expectancy by GDP category

The analysis shows a clear difference in average life expectancy between GDP categories. In 2023, countries in the High GDP category had an average life expectancy of 81.05 years, while countries in the Medium GDP category had an average of 74.31 years. Countries in the Low GDP category had the lowest average life expectancy, at 66.40 years.

This result suggests that higher GDP per capita is generally associated with higher life expectancy. However, this is an aggregated comparison and does not explain all differences between individual countries.
## 6. Population growth

The highest population growth between the first and latest available year was observed in the United Arab Emirates, Qatar, Western Sahara, Kuwait, Jordan, and Saudi Arabia.

The United Arab Emirates had the highest population growth in the result, increasing from 74,532 people in the first available year to 10,642,089 people in the latest available year. This represents growth of approximately 14,178.55%.

Several countries with the highest population growth are located in the Middle East. This may reflect a combination of natural population growth, migration, and economic development over time.
